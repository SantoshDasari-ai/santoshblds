In-vitro Pulsatile Flow Environment for Blood Clot Growth Characterization near Bileaflet MHVs (TGT 2.0)

Senior Design Project - In Progress

Background

Mechanical Heart Valves (MHVs) present a significant risk of thromboembolism in patients. While these implants are crucial for many patients, research into their thrombogenicity has been limited by inadequate in-vitro testing environments.

Previous research in the Cardio Lab utilized the 'Thrombogenicity Tester (TGT)' - a device that generated pulsatile flow in an enclosed loop containing an MHV and blood. However, this model had key limitations:

  • Horizontal orientation didn't reflect the heart's natural position
  • Gravity's influence on valve function was not properly simulated
  • Limited ability to prevent retrograde blood flow
  • Inadequate simulation of natural valve closure post-systole

Design Requirements

  • Flow rate range: 0-500 mL/min
  • Temperature control: 35-40°C
  • Pressure monitoring capabilities
  • Sterilization compatibility
  • Vertical orientation for proper gravity simulation
  • Enhanced retrograde flow prevention

Current TGT

Key Features

  • Horizontal orientation
  • Basic flow control system
  • Limited pressure monitoring
  • Simple temperature regulation

Limitations

  • Non-physiological valve orientation
  • Inadequate gravity effects simulation
  • Poor retrograde flow prevention
  • Limited control over valve closure dynamics
  • Basic data acquisition capabilities

TGT 2.0 - In Progress

Improvements

  • Vertical orientation for proper physiological simulation
  • Advanced flow control with PID system
  • Comprehensive pressure monitoring
  • Precise temperature control (35-40°C)
  • Enhanced retrograde flow prevention mechanism

Additional Features

  • Real-time data acquisition and logging
  • Modular design for easy maintenance
  • Improved sterilization compatibility
  • Advanced safety protocols and fail-safes
  • Enhanced sensor integration capabilities
